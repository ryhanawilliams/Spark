What Is Spark?

Spark Hackathon x Case Competition is a 24-hour innovation challenge where students come together to design, prototype, and pitch creative solutions to a real-world problem. By combining the hands-on building of a hackathon with the strategic thinking of a case competition, Spark pushes participants to go beyond coding and think deeply about why their solutions matter.

At Spark, teams of up to 4 are challenged with a real-life case, centred on social good, an issue that impacts communities locally or globally.

Spark is designed for everyone: developers, designers, entrepreneurs, and anyone excited to solve meaningful problems. It's a space where students work collaboratively, learn new skills, and innovate for causes that drive positive change.

FAQ

What is a hackathon? +

A hackathon is a fast-paced event where people come together to build creative projects usually over 12–36 hours. Participants form teams, brainstorm ideas, and develop solutions using coding, design, and problem-solving. It's a space to learn new skills, meet others in tech, and turn an idea into something real in a short amount of time.

What is a case competition? +

A case competition is an event where teams analyze a real-world business problem and present their solution to judges. Participants research, strategize, and build a clear, persuasive pitch all within a set time limit.

How does Spark combine a hackathon and case competition? +

Participants will analyze a real-world problem through a case, just like in a case competition, then build their proposed solution as they would in a hackathon, and finally pitch their product to a panel of judges.

Does Spark cost anything? +

Spark is completely free! All we ask is that you come ready to build :)

Do I need to come with a team? +

You can come solo or with a team. If you already have a team of 3-4 people, you can register with them! If you don't have a team there will be time before hacking to form teams or we can match you up with one before the event.
